# DeepTIPS

Activity-Guided Generative Discovery and Multidimensional Screening of Tyrosinase Inhibitory Peptides with Multiscale Biological Validation

Excessive melanogenesis caused by aberrant tyrosinase activity is closely associated with pigmentation disorders and remains a major target for the development of skin-whitening agents. Although peptides represent attractive alternatives to conventional small-molecule inhibitors due to their high specificity and biocompatibility, the efficient discovery of tyrosinase inhibitory peptides (TIPs) is limited by the vast sequence space and insufficient quantitative activity information. Here, we develop DeepTIPS, a computational–experimental strategy for the discovery and optimization of functional TIPs. By integrating IC50-based activity modeling, peptide sequence generation, and multi-parameter molecular screening, DeepTIPS enables systematic exploration of peptide chemical space toward highly active and developable candidates. Generated peptides were further evaluated based on inhibitory activity, cytocompatibility, cellular permeability, photostability, and molecular interactions with the tyrosinase catalytic center through docking and molecular dynamics simulations. This approach identified ten previously unexplored TIP candidates with favorable physicochemical properties and predicted binding characteristics. Experimental validation in B16 melanoma cells and zebrafish models confirmed their potent inhibition of melanogenesis and favorable biological compatibility, with representative peptides exhibiting consistent activity across in vitro and in vivo systems. These findings demonstrate that integrating quantitative activity landscapes with constrained peptide generation provides an effective route for discovering functional peptide molecules and offers new candidates for the development of next-generation skin-whitening agents.
